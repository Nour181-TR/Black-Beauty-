Black Beauty Story

Black Beauty is a classic novel written by Anna Sewell, published in 1877.The
story is narrated by the titular character, a highbred horse named Black Beauty,
who recounts his life experiences from his idyllic days as a colt on an English farm
to his struggles as a carriage horse in Londonand finally, his happy retirement in
the countryside.

  1. Kindness and Cruelty

    Black Beauty’s story is a poignant portrayal of the treatment of horses during the
    19th century. He describes his early life with kind masters, such as John Manly,
    who treated him with respect and care. However, as he is sold to successive
    owners, Black Beauty faces cruel and abusive treatment, including overwork, neglect, and physical punishment.

  2. Themes and Messages

    Through Black Beauty’s narrative, Sewell conveys important themes and
    messages about animal welfare, kindness, and compassion. The novel aims to
    “induce kindness, sympathy, and an understanding treatment of horses” and to
    raise awareness about the mistreatment of horses during that era.

  3. Key Characters

    * Black Beauty: The protagonist, a beautiful and gentle black horse who narrates the story.
    * John Manly: Black Beauty’s kind and gentle master at Birtwick Park.
    * Ginger: A spirited and lively mare who becomes Black Beauty’s companion.
    * Lady Waverley: A kind and compassionate owner who treats Black Beauty with respect and care.
